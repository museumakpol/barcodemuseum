Tokubetsu Keisatsutai

Deskripsi
Kemenangan Sekutu atas Jepang di berbagai wiiayah, membuat pertahanan Jepang di daerah-daerah pendudukan Asia Tenggara semakin kritis. Jepang segera merancang program Giyugun (tentara cadangan) dan Keisatsutai pada kesatuan kepolisian. Pada April 1944 Jepang membentuk satuan Polisi Istimewa atau Polisi Khusus, Tokubetsu Keisatsutai, sebagai bagian dari strategi perang Asia Timur Raya. Polisi Istimewa berdiri pada tiap karesidenan (Syu) di seluruh Jawa, Madura, dan Sumatera dengan pusat organisasi berada di masing-masing daerah. Kepolisian Jawa dan Madura berpusat di Jakarta dan Kepolisian untuk Timur Besar berpusat di Makassar.
Para pemuda bumiputera berusia minimal 17 tahun banyak mendaftar. Tiap hari mereka berlari 30 kilometer, baris-berbaris, berlatih judo, dan berlatih gulat. Para siswa juga belajar strategi perang, menganalisis serangan, dan menggunakan segala jenis persenjataan. Pendidikan Polisi Istimewa berlangsung setahun. Selepas itu berlanjut pendidikan militer khusus selama enam bulan.
Untuk menjadi instruktur bagi calon Polisi Istimewa, diberikan pendidikan selama tiga bulan. Materi yang diterima berkaitan dengan tugas dan kewajiban polisi, latihan kemiliteran, serta pendidikan dan latihan sebagai instruktur.
Sejak Jepang menyerah kepada Sekutu, seluruh satuan militer dan semimiliter bentukan Jepang dibubarkan. Kesatuan yang masih boleh memegang senjata adalah Tokubetsu Keisatsutai, cikal bakal Mobile Brigade/Mobrig (saat ini Brimob) berdiri pada 14 November 1946.

The Allied victory over Japan in various regions, made Japan's defense in the occupied areas of Southeast Asia increasingly critical. Japan immediately designed the Giyugun (reserve army) and Keisatsutai programs in the police force. In April 1944 Japan formed the Special Police or Special Police unit, Tokubetsu Keisatsutai, as part of its Greater East Asia war strategy.
The Special Police were established in each residency (Syu) throughout Java, Madura, and Sumatra with the center of the organization located in each region. The Java and Madura Police were based in Jakarta and the Police for the Great East were based in Makassar.
Many native youths aged at least 17 years have signed up. Every day they ran 30 kilometers, marched, practiced judo, and wrestling. Students also learnt war strategies, analyzed arracks, and used all kinds or weaponry. Special Police Education lasted a year. After that the education moved further to special military education for six months.
To become an instructor for the Special Police candidate, the cadres were given three months of education. The materials received were related to the duties and responsibilities of the police, military training, as well as education and training as instructors.
Since Japan surrendered to the Allies, all military and semi-military units formed by Japan were disbanded. The unit was still allowed to own weapon was Tokubetsu Keisatsutai, the forerunner of the Mobile Brigade/Mobrig (currently known as Brimob) was founded on November 14, 1946.
